AI Use Policy Guide

The MCN Admissions Team acknowledges that artificial intelligence tools are widely accessible and commonly used by students for writing support. This policy does not prohibit the responsible use of such tools. Instead, it establishes clear boundaries to ensure that all submitted applications reflect the applicant‚Äôs own thinking, experiences, and ideas.

‚úÖ AI may be used as a supplementary editing tool. Acceptable uses include correcting grammar, improving clarity, refining sentence flow, or adjusting structure. These forms of assistance are comparable to using proofreading software or seeking language support.

‚ùå AI may not be used to generate the substance of an application. This includes developing project ideas, constructing arguments, producing reflections, or shaping the intellectual core of responses. The originality, reasoning, and insight presented in the application must belong to the student.

When evaluating applications, reviewers must assess two dimensions:

  1. The nature of AI use: whether it appears limited to language refinement or extends to idea generation and substantive content.
  2. The extent of AI use: whether it appears in isolated sections or consistently throughout the application.

The purpose of this policy is to preserve authenticity and fairness while recognizing that language support tools may be used responsibly.

Red Flags and Green Flags

Reviewers should rely on pattern recognition across the full application rather than isolated phrases. The indicators below are intended to guide judgment, not replace it.

A. Assessing Whether AI Was Used for Substance or Only Language Refinement
Green Flags ‚Äî Likely Supplementary Editing Only Red Flags ‚Äî Likely Substantive AI Reliance
Clear references to personal experiences, communities, or institutions. Repeated restatement of the same idea using different synonyms without adding depth.
Specific examples with concrete details rather than abstract descriptions. Overuse of broad, vague, abstract terminology without specific examples.
Reflections that demonstrate nuance, self-awareness, or lessons learned. Highly polished but generic responses that could apply to any context.
Natural variation in tone and structure across responses. Uniform paragraph structure across all answers.
Minor imperfections or stylistic inconsistencies that reflect authentic writing. Lack of personal voice, local context, or concrete detail.
These features suggest that the applicant‚Äôs ideas are original, even if the language appears polished. These patterns may indicate that AI was used to generate or heavily shape the substance of the application.
B. Assessing Whether AI Was Used Throughout or in Limited Portions
Indicators of Limited Use Indicators of Extensive Use
One or two responses noticeably more refined than others. Consistently uniform tone across all responses.
Shifts in tone between sections. Similar sentence rhythm and structure throughout the application.
Mostly grounded, authentic responses with isolated formal passages. Multiple abstract and vague answers.
Lack of variation in vocabulary or expression style.
This pattern suggests that AI may have been used selectively for editing purposes. This pattern suggests sustained reliance rather than occasional editing support.

Reviewer Decision Framework

For each application, reviewers must select one of the following four options in the review sheet (the drop-down menu for the question, ‚ÄúOverall, do the applicant‚Äôs responses seem AI-generated?‚Äù):

  1. Option 1: Yes, the responses seem completely AI-generated and the ideas seem unoriginal.
  2. Option 2: Yes, AI has been used for the majority of the application but only to enhance language and grammar; the ideas seem original.
  3. Option 3: Yes, AI has been used for a short portion of the application but only to enhance language and grammar; the ideas seem original.
  4. Option 4: No, the student‚Äôs responses seem completely original without any apparent use of AI.

Decision Guide

Reviewers must base their selection on the overall pattern of the application rather than a single sentence or paragraph.

Examples

Example 1 ‚Äî Likely Fully AI-Generated (Select Option 1)
"My initiative seeks to create sustainable and transformative impact by empowering marginalized communities through innovative and scalable solutions that foster systemic change and long-term resilience."
This response relies heavily on abstract language and broad claims without concrete detail or personal grounding.

Example 2 ‚Äî Majority Language Enhancement (Select Option 2)
"In my second year at university, I noticed that many first-generation students struggled to navigate academic requirements. I plan to create a peer mentorship network that offers structured workshops and simplified guides to reduce confusion and improve retention."
The idea is specific and grounded. If similar polish appears consistently across the application, Option 2 should be selected.

Example 3 ‚Äî Limited Editing Support (Select Option 3)
‚ÄúI started a small tutoring group after realizing many of my classmates were struggling with calculus. At first, we simply met to review assignments together, but over time it developed into a more organized peer-support network that improved both confidence and performance. Through this experience, I came to better understand the importance of accessible academic support structures within university communities.‚Äù
This paragraph is clearly rooted in a specific personal experience, but the structure and phrasing are noticeably more polished than the applicant‚Äôs typical informal tone elsewhere.

Example 4 ‚Äî Fully Original (Select Option 4)
"When heavy rains damaged several homes in my area, I organized classmates to collect supplies. We did not have a plan at first, and we made mistakes, but the experience showed me how unprepared we were. That is why I want to create a campus emergency response training group."
This response demonstrates specificity, lived experience, and authentic reflection.

Final Guidance

AI detection requires careful judgment. Polished writing alone should not be penalized, and such applications must not be rejected. The determining factor is originality.

If the ideas, reasoning, and insight clearly originate from the applicant, minor editing assistance is acceptable. If originality appears compromised or the application lacks authentic substance, it must be marked accordingly.

This policy is intended to ensure fairness, protect authenticity, and uphold the integrity of the Millennium Fellowship admissions process.

Official Evaluation Rubric

Part 1: Project Description & SDG

Clarity & Problem Identification (1.1 - 1.3)

5 - Highly Agree Candidate effectively identifies a problem that relates to at least one SDG and offers a compelling response through their project. Description is highly clear and uses simple language as if explaining to a friend. Avoids all buzzwords.

4 - Agree Candidate identifies a problem related to at least one SDG, but with some lack of clarity or specificity. They offer a reasonably compelling response to that problem. Description is clear and easy to understand. Mostly uses simple language, though it may include 1-2 minor technical terms that do not hinder comprehension.

3 - Unsure Description is generally understandable but lacks consistency in clarity. Uses some buzzwords or technical terms that may confuse a non-expert reader.

2 - Disagree Candidate's problem statement lacks clarity or fails to address a significant issue related at least one SDG or their project is not a compelling response to the problem identified. Description is vague or poorly structured. Overuses buzzwords or unexplained technical language, making it difficult for a non-expert to follow.

1 - Highly Disagree Candidate does not clearly identify a problem or fails to relate it to at least one SDG or offers a poor response to the problem identified. Phrases answers in future uncertainity like "can do", "we can try", "will try to". Description is unclear, confusing, or inaccessible. Heavily relies on jargon and buzzwords, with little to no effort to simplify or explain ideas.

Number of Lives Impacted (1.9 & 2)

5 - Highly Agree Candidate provides a specific, well-reasoned number based on concrete project plans and target groups. They demonstrate understanding of potential impact limitations and provide justification for their estimate.

4 - Agree Candidate provides a reasonable number with some explanation of how they arrived at it. However, they may lack details about target groups or project reach.

3 - Unsure Candidate expresses uncertainity about number of lives impacted or avoids giving speific estimate. They may need furhter guidance or clarification on how to quantify impact.

2 - Disagree Candidate provides an unrealistic number without justification or contradicts other information in their application. They may need to refine their project goals or impact measurement strategy.

1 - Highly Disagree Candidate claims their project will have no positive impact or even suggests potential negative consequences. This raises concerns about project feasibility and alignment with program goals.

SDG & UNAI Principles (2.1 & 2.2)

5 - Highly Agree Candidate clearly identifies a specific SDG and provides a convincing explanation of how their work directly addresses its core objectives and targets. They demonstrate a deep understandng of the selected SDG and its relevance to their work. Candidate clearly and explicitly demonstrates a strong connection between their work and a specific UN Academic Impact Principle, citing relevant details and objectives. They show a deep understanding of the principle and how their work contributes to its goals.

4 - Agree Candidate identifies a relevant SDG and makes a reasonable connection to their work, but the explanation lacks depth or specificity. They demonstrate some understanding of the chosen SDG but could elaborate on the specific targets their work addresses. Candidate acknowledges the UN Academic Impact Principles or is unable to clearly articulate a connection between their work and a UN Academic Impact Principle, but the explanation lacks specifics or depth. They could benefit from providing more concrete examples or elaborating on their understanding.

3 - Unsure Candidate identifies a connection to the SDGs but is unsure which one best aligns with their work or provides a vague connection. They may need further guidance or information to identify the most relevant SDG. Candidate is unsure of the specific UN Academic Impact connection between their work and any principle. They may need further resources or guidance.

2 - Disagree Candidate identifies an SDG that is not directly relevant to their work or fails to explain the connection convincingly. They demonstrate limited understanding of the SDGs or their relationship to their work. Candidate does not see a connection between their work and any UN Academic Impact Principles, potentially due to a misunderstanding of the principles or their own work's focus. Clarification and additional information may be needed.

1 - Highly Disagree Candidate's work has no connection to any SDG or actively rejects the relelance of SDGs despite potential connections. They demonstrate a clear misunderstanding or disinterest in the SDGs. Candidate's work explicitly refutes any connection between their work and the UN Academic Impact Principles, potentially demonstrating a lack of awareness or alignment with the goals. Further exploration of their motivations and understanding is required.

Part 1: Proximity to Issue

Community Connection (2.2)

5 - Highly Agree Candidate Clearly demonstrates strong personal connection to the community, actively involved as a member or through deep, impactful relationships. (To note: Extensive personal stories, concrete examples of involvement, clear understanding of community dynamics).

4 - Agree Candidate Shows some connection to the community, either through past experiences or ongoing interactions (Examples of involvement, understanding of community needs, but lacks depth or current engagement).

3 - Unsure Unclear level of connection to the community, lacks specific details or offers conflicting information (Vague statements, limited understanding of community dynamics, no clear evidence of engagement).

2 - Disagree Candidate does not demonstrate any clear connection to the community, suggests involvement was superficial or not impactful. (No examples, dismissive language about the community, prioritizes external perspectives).

1 - Highly Disagree Candidate has no connection to the community, expresses biased vies and demonstrates lack of understanding and empathy. (Harmful stereotypes, insensitive language, clear disregard for community perspectives).

Part 2: You as a Leader

Personal Motivation - Thoughtful/Emotional Investment (Q1)

5 - Highly Agree The candidate demonstrates a clear and compelling personal story that explains their interest in social impact.

4 - Agree The candidate shares a personal experience that deeply connects to their chosen social issue.

3 - Unsure The candidate mentions personal experiences, but the link to social impact motivation is not fully developed.

2 - Disagree The candidate mentions personal experiences, but they are not relevant to their chosen social issue.

1 - Highly Disagree The candidate does not mention any personal experiences or motivations for their interest in social impact.

Personal Motivation - Genuine Passion (Q1)

5 - Highly Agree Clearly articulates a deep, personal "why" behind their work. The passion feels lived-in and authentic.

4 - Agree Explains interest in the fellowship and its social impact clearly and sincerely. Shows strong, genuine motivation, though the personal connection may not be deeply explored.

3 - Unsure States reasons for applying that show some interest and alignment with the fellowship, but the personal "why" remains surface-level or underdeveloped.

2 - Disagree Reasons for applying feel generic or vague. Motivation appears weak or unclear, with limited connection to social impact or personal values.

1 - Highly Disagree Motivation is unclear or primarily focused on r√©sum√©-building, credentials, or external rewards, with little to no indication of personal or social commitment.

MCN Values & Fellowship (Q2)

5 - Highly Agree The candidate articulates how their own values align with the program's values and aslo outlines specific ways they will engage with the program's resources and opportunities.

4 - Agree The candidate mentions the program's values and also contributing to the program, but the connection to their own motivation is unclear and their plans are vague or unspecific.

3 - Unsure The candidate mentions the program, but their focus is primarily on personal benefits or outcomes and focuses primarily on receiving benefits from the program with little mention of contribution.

Leadership & Teamwork - Empathy & Humility (Q3)

5 - Highly Agree The candidate clearly describes their role and responsibilities as a leader and their actions show genuine consideration for others' feelings and perspectives.

4 - Agree The candidate mentions a leadership experience and mentions relevant MCN value, but the details are vague or incomplete and their demonstration is unclear or underdeveloped.

3 - Unsure The candidate mentions leadership, but it's unclear if they were in a true leadership position and the actions are primarily focused on achieving results or gaining recognition.

Community Mindset (Q3)

5 - Highly Agree Expresses a strong desire to collaborate with and learn from other Fellows. Values the "we" over the "I".

4 - Agree Recognizes the importance of the fellowship community and mentions specific ways they hope to engage.

3 - Unsure The candidate's response does not reflect their ideas on teamwork and collaboration.

2 - Disagree Focuses almost entirely on personal gain or individual project success.
